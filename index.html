Mathematical Timeline

The evolution of human mathematical understanding

~3000 BC

Ancient Mathematics

Babylonians develop place-value number system and solve quadratic equations. Egyptians use geometry for pyramid construction.

~600 BC

Greek Mathematics

Thales and Pythagoras establish mathematical proof. Euclid writes "Elements," the most influential mathematics textbook ever.

~628 AD

Indian Mathematics

Brahmagupta develops rules for zero and negative numbers. Decimal system and trigonometry flourish.

~820 AD

Islamic Mathematics

Al-Khwarizmi develops algebra. Islamic mathematicians preserve and extend Greek knowledge.

1637

Coordinate Geometry

Descartes merges algebra and geometry, creating analytic geometry and the Cartesian coordinate system.

1687

Calculus Revolution

Newton publishes Principia, demonstrating calculus's power in describing natural phenomena.

1799

Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

Gauss proves every polynomial has at least one complex root, unifying algebra and analysis.

1905

Modern Physics

Einstein's relativity theories revolutionize space, time, and mathematics' role in physics.
